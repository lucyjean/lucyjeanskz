Stray Kids Overview

Stray Kids is an eight-member K-pop group formed by JYP Entertainment in 2017, known for their high-energy performances, self-produced music, and passionate fanbase called STAY.

Identity & Sound

3RACHA (Bang Chan, Changbin, Han) lead production; sound blends hip‑hop, EDM, rock; themes: identity, resilience, independence.

Departure of Kim Woo-jin

Woojin, whose full name is Kim Woo-jin, was one of the original nine members of Stray Kids, serving as a main vocalist. In October 2019, JYP Entertainment announced that Woojin had left the group and terminated his contract due to personal circumstances. The announcement was brief and did not go into detail, and no further official explanation was given by JYP or Woojin at that time. His departure came just before the release of Stray Kids’ mini-album Clé: LEVANTER, which led to adjustments in the album’s release schedule and the re-recording of tracks without his vocals, as well as major anxiety and uncertnity about the performace of Stray Kids without him. However, Stray Kids popularity and revenue has only grown (see graph for more details).
